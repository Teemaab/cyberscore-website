# CyberScore — Product Marketing Context

## Product
**CyberScore** is a cyberhygiene platform for Windows workstations.
An agent (.exe) runs locally on the machine, scores it 0–100 across two dimensions
(Technique + Usage/Behaviour), and sends the result to a centralised DSI dashboard.
No server setup. No expertise required. The employee double-clicks, sees their score
in under 3 minutes. The IT manager sees the full fleet in real time.

## Primary Persona — IT Manager / DSI
- Title: Responsable IT, DSI, Directeur Informatique
- Context: manages 10–100 Windows PCs, no dedicated security team, limited budget
- Pain: doesn't know which machines are vulnerable TODAY, can't afford a full-time cyber expert
- Trigger: audit requirement, incident scare, regulatory pressure (e.g. BCEAO, ISO27001 aspirations)
- Language: French-speaking, may speak Wolof/Bambara/Dioula natively
- Decision process: shows the tool to their DG/CEO; price approved by DG
- Key objections:
  1. "Is my data safe if it goes to a cloud?" → local mode answer
  2. "Will my employees be able to use this?" → 3-minute double-click answer
  3. "Is this adapted to African context?" → French, FCFA pricing, local case studies

## Secondary Persona — CEO / DG of SME
- Title: Directeur Général, PDG, Gérant
- Context: 10–200 employees, heard about a ransomware attack on a peer, worried
- Pain: no visibility, no one to call, doesn't want to hire an expert just to "see the situation"
- Decision style: relationship-driven, references from known contacts matter most
- Trigger: banker/auditor/partner asked about cybersecurity posture
- Key message: "3 minutes, un score clair, en français — vous savez enfin où vous en êtes."

## Key Differentiators (vs. international SaaS tools)
1. **French language** — entire UX, reports, AI recommendations: 100% français
2. **FCFA pricing** — local currency, no hidden EUR/USD conversion surprises
3. **Local / offline mode** — critical differentiator for banks, governments, telcos
4. **No infrastructure** — competitor tools (Qualys, Tenable, SentinelOne) require servers, agents, expertise
5. **3-minute time-to-insight** — vs. weeks for a traditional audit (500k–5M FCFA)
6. **Designed for African SME reality** — AD domain AND workgroup, no MDM required

## Core Message Architecture
- **Problem**: PME africaines ont des postes vulnérables et ne le savent pas
- **Solution**: CyberScore donne un score de sécurité clair en 3 minutes, sans expert
- **Proof**: Score 0–100, rapport PDF, tableau de bord DSI, recommandations IA en français
- **Risk removal**: Pas d'engagement, paiement après validation, mode local disponible

## Conversion Context
- Traffic source: LinkedIn (DSI/IT), WhatsApp sharing (DG referrals), Google Search
- Conversion goal: form submission → discovery call with the CyberScore team
- NOT a self-serve checkout — sales-assisted, no credit card at signup
- Avg. deal cycle: 1–3 weeks (SME); 3–8 weeks (government/bank)

## Trust Signals That Matter in This Market
1. Specific numbers (postes analysés, scores réels from real clients)
2. Named city + sector (not just "Dakar" but "Cabinet comptable, Dakar")
3. Local testimonials with recognisable sectors (banque, assurance, ONG)
4. Institutional logos if available
5. Sector familiarity: "Nous travaillons avec des équipes IT au Sénégal, Mali, Côte d'Ivoire..."

## CRO Priorities (in order)
1. Risk-reversal copy under every CTA (commitment anxiety)

3. Testimonial credibility (specific metrics, company type, star ratings)
4. Pricing CTA clarity (demo, not purchase)
